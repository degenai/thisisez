Batch 3, the death eras (decided 2026-07-15): we worried the sandbox (2012–2018, a bull era where every dip recovered) had taught us a monoculture — "wild pairs pay more" might just mean "wildness was good when nothing died." So we annexed two eras where things actually die and re-fought everyone. Verdict: the two champions are sign-stable everywhere — γ and Anti-Kinship pay in the GFC and the dot-com bust too — while Title-Change, Breath, and DD-γ all flipped sign where death is real and are benched as regime-bound. And the corpse belt stayed vacant even with a severity fitness (flagging genuine −5%/yr disasters). Best finding money can't buy: pair-demons die of dispersion, not crashes. The dot-com collapse — the scariest chart in the library — produced 20× fewer pair catastrophes than the GFC, because everything fell tethered together; it's the eras when assets decouple that kill. The radar's smoke detector, when it's finally built, will be listening for divorce, not for fire.
