A portfolio is something you work, not something you hold. Shannon's Demon: assets that each go nowhere but wander, rebalanced on a schedule, compound a positive growth rate out of pure volatility. The money is never in a sleeve — it lives in the spread between sleeves. Harvest it by trimming what rose and buying what fell.
The operator, not the rentier
An ETF is congealed multiplicity — a hundred firms in one handle. The rentier re-atomizes it: relates to the bundle as a single object to hold or flee, and reaches for a 200-day moving average as his only defense. The operator keeps it as one node in an ensemble and works the edges between nodes. Composability is the whole game — and it is also a class position. The demon lives in the spread between nodes, never in a node.
Excess growth rate of a pair ≈ ½ · var(return spread) · 252 = annualized pairwise demon yield. This one scalar is the heat map the whole apparatus is built around.

The seven laws
I · Prime the Spring
Rebalance INTO drawdowns
Each buy of a crashing sleeve is a low-basis, high-share lot. The discipline loads the spring under tension; the reversion releases it. Drawdown isn't the enemy — it's the raw material.
II · The Bite
Trim rich → buy poor, $1 units
Sell one executable dollar-unit from the most-over-target sleeve, buy it into the most-under. Human-runnable quanta, no fractional-penny fantasy.
III · The Plateau
Bite size is a drawdown & tax lever, not a returns lever
1%–5% of book all return the same. Size controls drawdown and taxable events — and it's cadence-conditional (see below).
IV · The Free Demon
Contributions deploy dispersion-first
Route every drip to the most-underweight sleeve, never pro-rata, never selling. Free rebalancing, zero taxable events. On a quiet day the paycheck is the best rebalance of the day.
V · The Tax Knob
Widen the band, not the returns
5% → 10% completion band = identical returns, ~4× fewer taxable events. The band is a tax dial.
VI · Demon Routing
Advisory only
Route through the highest spread-variance pair when several qualify. Under resampling it's a coin flip (50.4%). Tiebreaker, never an oracle.
VII · The Gate
OFF
The 200-day TQQQ trend filter is a passive holder's tool. It cost ~2% of terminal wealth in every window to shave the productive drawdown the harvest is paid to take. Anti-spring. Dropped.
Law III in one table — coarseness buys attention-slack
How often you lookBite sizeWhy
Automated / strong (≤3 bites/day)~1.0–1.5% (≈125bps)Frequent small corrections keep drift tight.
Manual / realistic (~1 look/day)2.0–5.0%A single coarse bite corrects hard when you do look. Fine bites at low cadence post the worst drawdown of any policy.
Key substitution: every-2-days @ 500bps ≈ daily @ 200bps. Going coarser lets you check the book half as often for the same drawdown. Ship the band, not an "ideal point" — the plateau is flat, so a round number inside it is the anti-overfit choice.
The Demon Ladder — demons by order
Demons rank by order = the compositional scale of the fixed committee they harvest. 1st order = two-sleeve pairs (7,600 swept here). 2nd = triads, 3rd = quads, up to n-sleeve. The live book — 7 equal sleeves — is a high-order demon, and it's surprisingly hard to beat. That's a finding, not a boast. Climb the Ladder to see one sleeve bolted on at a time.
Cautionary tale from the first-order sweep: YANG/YINN topped the demon-yield leaderboard at +34%/yr — on a +0.2% CAGR. The metric doing the work. Exhaustive search finds coincidences by construction. Which is exactly why we built the tournament below.
The Tournament of Power — twelve universes
500 bootstrap worlds sound like a lot, but they're all children of one dataset — the same market physics reshuffled. A demon that survives them has only survived one reality's statistics. Real robustness means surviving structurally different physics. So the tournament runs across twelve universe-types, 500 worlds each — 6,000 worlds. A demon that fails a universe is erased from it. The champion survives all twelve.

The referee is not the in-sample leaderboard. Rank thousands of demons by raw yield and you just crown the prettiest coincidence. The judge is survival across the universes. The research question: do the same demons win everywhere — structural determinism — or does each universe crown its own — universe-dependence? Either answer is a finding.
Rung four — the protean portfolio (the last frontier)
Everything above holds the asset universe fixed and works the relationships inside it. Rung four lets the membership itself mutate — add, drop, swap sleeves. The operator mindset one level higher: composing the node set, not just the edges. It's where backtests lie hardest (survivorship, look-ahead), so it comes only after the tournament maps the static topography. The math is already waiting — it's exactly what the universal-portfolio literature (Cover; online portfolio selection) is the theory of.

Methodology — the epistemics
  • Ledger over theory. The broker's confirmed fills outrank every backtest and every doc, including this one. Reconcile to 6 decimals.
  • Parity gates. Before any comparison, the unchanged code path is re-run and hash-compared byte-for-byte. Every feature ships inert-by-default and provably doesn't exist until asked for.
  • Bootstrap as judge. Findings must survive resampling — and, going forward, the twelve universes. In-sample wins are hypotheses, not results.
  • Selection-bias honesty. Exhaustive search over thousands of demons produces winners by construction. Log what was dropped; never quote a top row as doctrine.
  • The primitive stack. Stdlib Python, vanilla HTML/JS, hand-rolled dashboards. Libraries, not frameworks. The corner test applied to technology: don't hold what everyone can farm.
